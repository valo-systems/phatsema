# Meeting Context

**Research status:** Initial discovery  
**Meeting date:** 18 July 2026  
**Website research date:** 18 July 2026  
**Scope:** Agape Water commercial context and Phatsema back-office discovery

## What was confirmed in the meeting

> **Confirmed by meeting**

- The meeting was initially expected to focus on Agape Water.
- The client instead prioritised Phatsema, described in the meeting as its mining and supplier company.
- Phatsema wants a back-office system that can track inventory across all its sites.
- The commercial work for Agape Water and Phatsema should be billed through one invoice.

The single-invoice request is recorded as a commercial instruction only. It does not establish that Agape Water and either Phatsema business are the same legal entity, share a VAT registration, or should share accounting records.

## Public businesses identified

> **Published on website**

Two related public websites were identified:

1. [Phatsema Projects & Supplies](https://phatsema.com/) describes plant hire, material handling, engineering, industrial services, fabrication, logistics, crushing and screening, and project supplies.
2. [Phatsema Mining](https://phatsemamining.co.za/) describes dust suppression products, yellow-machinery rentals, loading and haulage, mining hoses, and related projects and supplies.

The Projects & Supplies website links directly to the Mining website, and the Mining website links back to Projects & Supplies. Both use the same physical address in Brits and name the same CEO.

## Working interpretation

> **Inference**

The proposed back office may need to support more than conventional saleable inventory. The public offerings suggest possible requirements for:

- Stocked chemicals and components.
- Site consumables.
- Fabricated-to-order items and work in progress.
- Company-owned machinery and attachments.
- Hired or rented assets.
- Equipment undergoing repair or rebuild.
- Client-owned machinery held for service.
- Stock issued to remote operating sites.

These are discovery hypotheses, not approved system requirements.

## Commercial and governance questions

> **Requires confirmation**

1. What are the exact registered legal names and company-registration numbers for Agape Water, Phatsema Mining, and Phatsema Projects & Supplies?
2. Which entity will contract with and pay the software provider?
3. Who should the combined invoice be addressed to?
4. Are the entities registered for VAT, and may their work legally appear on one tax invoice?
5. Should the single invoice contain separate line items, purchase-order references, cost centres, or project codes for Agape Water and Phatsema?
6. Are Phatsema Mining and Phatsema Projects & Supplies separate legal entities, divisions, trading names, or brands?
7. Will the back office serve only Phatsema, or may Agape Water later use the same platform?
8. Who is the executive sponsor, operational owner, and day-to-day decision-maker for the back-office project?

## Immediate success criterion

The next discovery session should leave the project team with:

- A confirmed entity and billing structure.
- A list of operating sites and stock-holding locations.
- Agreed inventory categories and ownership rules.
- A map of the current receiving, issuing, transfer, return, rental, and stock-count processes.
- Named users, approvers, reports, and integrations required for an initial release.
