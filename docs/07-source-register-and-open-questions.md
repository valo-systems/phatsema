## Content quality and consistency findings

> **Published on website**

### Entity and offering overlap

1. The two websites cross-link and share a CEO and physical address, but do not explain the legal relationship between the businesses.
2. Loading and haulage appears on both websites with overlapping copy.
3. Projects and supplies is presented on `phatsema.com` and linked as an offering from the Mining website.
4. The Projects & Supplies About page mentions dust suppression, while detailed dust products appear on the Mining website.
5. The Projects & Supplies footer does not consistently list all homepage services; Loading & Haulage is omitted from the product/offering footer list.

### Contact-link problems

1. Multiple “Get in contact” links on `phatsema.com` use the malformed address `infophatsema.co.za`.
2. The displayed `lebo@phatsema.co.za` footer address was observed linking to `ebo@phatsema.co.za`.
3. Several Projects & Supplies service pages route enquiries to `info@phatsema-mining.co.za`.
4. Phatsema Mining's contact page lists the main number, while other named contacts appear only in website footers.
5. Mining customer-reference links point to a JDC Digital brochure URL rather than an obvious Phatsema reference document.

### Incomplete, placeholder, or stale content

1. The Mining Solutions page contains only the heading “Solutions.”
2. The Mining newsletter contains a default WordPress “Hello world!” post.
3. The Projects & Supplies newsletter contains only one listed networking article in the reviewed content.
4. The Projects & Supplies About page displays labels for years established, projects, clients, and employees without visible figures.
5. A CSI golf-day entry still contains the placeholder text `[insert date]`.
6. The Careers page listed a Qualified Mechanics vacancy closing on 14 July 2026, which had passed by the 18 July 2026 research date.

### Copy and data-quality issues

1. Website text contains spelling and grammar errors, including “nees,” “zero-hars,” “posistion,” and inconsistent capitalisation.
2. The school name appears as both Seroopata and Seroophata Primary School.
3. Several CSI farm-excursion stories appear to cover similar or repeated events.
4. The Dust Suppression page repeats some benefits and the 53.8% dust-fall result.
5. The Steel Services page uses the same description for plasma cutting and profile cutting, which may be incorrect.
6. The Projects & Supplies and Mining websites use different service-grouping and naming conventions.

### Unsupported or insufficiently qualified claims

1. Dust-control performance percentages do not include visible methodology or supporting technical reports.
2. The machine-rebuilding page's “up to 50%” saving lacks a published basis.
3. OEM-level restoration is claimed without identifying OEM authorisations or standards.
4. Agricultural regulatory compliance is claimed without naming the relevant registrations or authorities.
5. GRDC S10+B radiation-shielding statements require technical, safety, and regulatory evidence.
6. Cross-border operating claims do not identify current facilities, licences, projects, or local entities.

## Prioritised questions for the next client session

### Priority 0 — Decisions required before solution design

> **Requires confirmation**

1. What are the registered names, registration numbers, and VAT numbers of Agape Water, Phatsema Mining, and Phatsema Projects & Supplies?
2. What is the legal and operational relationship between the three?
3. Which entity is the contracting customer, and how must the combined invoice be structured?
4. Which entity or entities and operating sites are included in the back-office first release?
5. Who is the executive sponsor, product owner, operational process owner, and acceptance signatory?
6. Provide the complete list of stock-holding sites, warehouses, workshops, yards, and mobile stores.
7. What does the client mean by “inventory”: saleable goods, consumables, raw material, work in progress, machinery, rental assets, client assets, or all of these?

1. Demonstrate the current purchase-to-receipt, issue, transfer, return, count, and adjustment processes.

3. Identify all user roles, approvals, transaction volumes, and reporting needs.
4. Confirm units of measure and all batch, expiry, serial, registration, meter, and service requirements.
5. Explain fabrication, machine-rebuild, bucket-repair, and client-owned-equipment processes.
6. Explain machinery-rental contracts, dispatch, inspections, billing measures, maintenance, and returns.
7. Identify the finance, procurement, fleet, HR, or telematics systems that must remain sources of truth.
8. Confirm connectivity and offline needs at every site.
9. Define first-release success measures and the expected delivery timeline.

## Research handover summary

### Known

- The meeting established a need for central, multi-site Phatsema inventory visibility.
- The client asked for Agape Water and Phatsema work to appear on one commercial invoice.
- Publicly, Phatsema covers products, chemicals, fabrication, plant, rentals, logistics, repairs, automation, and on-site services.
- Two cross-linked Phatsema websites share leadership and a public address.

### Inferred

- The future operating model may need legal entity, site, warehouse, project, job, ownership, custody, and asset-status dimensions.
- Inventory and fixed/rental/client assets cannot safely be represented by one undifferentiated stock record.
- Remote operations may require mobile or offline workflows.

### Unknown

- Legal structure, first-release scope, complete site list, users, volumes, workflows, integrations, controls, reporting, budget, and timeline.
- Whether Agape Water will use the system or is relevant only to contracting and billing.
- Which website claims and contacts are current and formally approved.
