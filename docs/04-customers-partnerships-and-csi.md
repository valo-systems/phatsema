## Published customer evidence

> **Published on website**

The Phatsema Mining website publishes a testimonial attributed to **Bertus van der Merwe, Group COO**. It states that the customer's organisation had used GRDC S10 for haul-road dust suppression since June 2022.

The testimonial claims:

- Improved dust control, visibility, and road safety.
- Reliable product performance.
- Responsive technical and customer support.
- Easier application and improved durability.
- Lower maintenance requirements.
- Increased operational efficiency and cost savings.
- Improved working conditions.

The source page also publishes customer-reference names and roles for Bertus van der Merwe, Leo Erlank, and Eugene Viljoen. Their direct telephone numbers are not reproduced here.

**Sources:** [Phatsema Mining homepage](https://phatsemamining.co.za/); [Customer References](https://phatsemamining.co.za/customer-references/)  
**Accessed:** 18 July 2026

> **Requires confirmation**

- The customer organisation is not clearly identified in the testimonial text captured from the website.
- The customer-reference page links the named references to an unrelated JDC Digital brochure URL, which appears to be a website configuration error.
- Permission to reuse the testimonial, names, and claims in proposals or system seed data must be confirmed.
- Supporting product-performance evidence should be requested independently.

## Agape Water relationship

> **Confirmed by meeting**

Agape Water and Phatsema were represented in the same client conversation, and the client requested one invoice for the work covering both.

> **Published on website**

The Projects & Supplies CSI page says Phatsema and Agape Water jointly support the Bobby Motaung Foundation. It describes the relationship as a collaboration based on community upliftment and shared values.

**Source:** [Phatsema Corporate Social Investment](https://phatsema.com/csi/)  
**Accessed:** 18 July 2026

> **Requires confirmation**

The public partnership and combined-invoice request do not establish common ownership, a parent-subsidiary relationship, shared tax registration, or permission to share operational data.

## Data relevance to the back office

> **Inference**

CSI and sponsorship work may create separate procurement, donation-stock, event-asset, budget, approval, and reporting requirements. These should not be mixed with commercial inventory unless the client confirms that the same stores and processes are used.

Potential discovery areas include:

- Stock purchased for donation.
- Sponsored goods supplied by partners.
- Event equipment and promotional material.
- Approval and budget ownership.
- Recipient acknowledgement and proof of delivery.
- Reporting by business entity, initiative, and financial period.
