> **Published on website**

- Reduce water consumption, application frequency, fuel use, vehicle running costs, dust fall, airborne dust readings, and carbon footprint.
- Improve road-surface stability, visibility, safety, and operational efficiency.
- Require fewer applications than traditional water-based suppression.

It publishes the following performance figures:

- 50% less application frequency.
- 50.2% less water usage.
- 53.8% less dust fall.
- 47.3% lower PDR readings.
- 50% lower vehicle running cost.
- 53.6% less fuel usage.
- 53.6% lower carbon footprint.

> **Requires confirmation**

These figures are marketing claims. Before using them in proposals, product master data, compliance reporting, or client-facing material, obtain the test methodology, baseline, sample size, test site, dates, units, independent verification, and supporting technical documentation.

## Inventory interpretation

> **Inference**

The catalogue suggests at least six operational record types:

1. **Saleable stock:** Chemicals, hoses, pipes, fittings, and completed manufactured products.
2. **Consumables and raw materials:** Steel, HDPE inputs, welding consumables, repair parts, and chemical-handling components.
3. **Fabricated-to-order work:** Chutes, bins, trailers, hoses, and pipe systems tied to jobs or customer orders.
4. **Company-owned assets:** Machinery, trailers, tanks, PLCs, tools, and attachments.
5. **Rental assets:** Equipment issued under hire contracts and subject to inspections, meter readings, service intervals, and returns.
6. **Client-owned service items:** Buckets and machines accepted for repair or rebuilding.

The client must validate these categories before they become system requirements.
